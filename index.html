The Venezuelan Enterprise: Current Situation, Challenges and Opportunities

Gathering information on the private sector in Venezuela by applying the Enterprise Survey. Tasks and responsibilities included engaging with different industry stakeholders to gather contact information from relevant companies, contacting the selected companies to urge them to complete the survey, and providing regular updates on the data collection status.

  • Client: Inter-American Development Bank (IADB)
  • Category: Economics, Venezuela
See final report

Action plan to manage State Owned Enterprises in Venezuela

Developing an action plan to take control and manage State Owned Enterprises in anticipation of a possible change of government. Tasks and responsibilities included the identification of different legal regimes under which companies were and could be controlled, the definition of the scope of companies to be included, and the setting of plan priorities under different time frames.

  • Client: National Assembly of Venezuela
  • Category: Economics, Venezuela

Identification of investment opportunities in Venezuela

Identifying investment opportunities in key economic sectors like telecommunications, consumer goods, and financial markets. Tasks and responsibilities included mapping relevant companies in each sector, describing the potential market size and financial information of the companies, and identifying useful legal frameworks for investment.

  • Client: National Council for the Promotion of Investment (CONAPRI)
  • Category: Economics, Venezuela

Research on the use of open source software and Onderweg in Nederland (ODiN) travel survey data to calibrate activity-based travel demand models for the Netherlands, with potential applications on the arena of sustainable mobility.

  • Category: Transport, Modelling, Travel demand, Data analysis

Transport emissions accounting

CountEmissions EU

Assessing policy proposals for a harmonized framework for transport emissions accounting in the EU for all modes of transportation with the goal of making the information available and comparable to enable future transport decarbonization policies. Tasks and responsibilities include aiding in the execution of stakeholder engagement activities and performing data analyses on their results. The policy proposal that stemmed from this impact assessment was included in the greening freight package of laws proposed by the European Commission.

  • Client: European Commission (DG MOVE)
  • Category: European Union, Transport, Decarbonization
See publication

Ship Recycling Regulation

Assessing the suitability of currently existing ship recycling regulations and their effectiveness in reducing the negative environmental and social impacts linked to the recycling of EU-flagged ships. Responsibilities and tasks include modeling and data analysis on current ship recycling and proposed policies.

  • Client: European Commission (DG ENV)
  • Category: Shipping, Maritime transport, Circular Economy

Train drivers' training and certification

Revision of the Train Drivers' Directive

Revising the certification of train drivers aimed to enhance and facilitate their mobility across the rail network of the EU, as well as easing their assignment to operations in various Member States (for example, cross-border operations). Responsibilities and tasks include interpreting the data collected through stakeholder engagement and assessing the impacts of a subset of policy measures regarding the harmonization of train driver training and certification.

  • Client: European Commission (DG MOVE)
  • Category: European Union, Railway transport, Cross-border transport
See project

Techno-economic analysis for the energy transition of the EU fisheries and aquaculture sector

Analizing possible routes to achieve decarbonization of the European fisheries and aquaculture sector in the medium and long term in line with European carbon neutrality ambitions, with special attention to the impacts of recent crises on the sector, such as the COVID19 pandemic and the energy crisis. Responsibilities and tasks include planning and executing stakeholder consultation and analyzing the performance and current situation of the European fishing fleet to provide a point of reference to measure the decarbonization options.

  • Client: European Climate, Infrastructure and Environment Executive Agency (CINEA)
  • Category: Maritime transport, Fishing, Decarbonization
See publication

Readiness for regional integration of trucking in South Asia

Bangladesh, India, and Nepal

Project with the aim to facilitate regional trade and transport through an efficient implementation of the Motor Vehicle Agreement in Bangladesh, India, and Nepal. The objective of this project is to support this process providing pre-feasibility and analytical studies to better inform the strategic decisions of the countries involved. Tasks and responsibilities included describing the state of play of the Nepalese trucking industry, the barriers for implementation of the agreement (and regional connectivity), and providing recommendations to address them and build capacity.

  • Client: World Bank
  • Category: Road transport, Cross-border transport, Asia
See related publication

Study on the Analysis, Prevention and Management of Air Traffic Controller (ATCO) fatigue

Project with the aim of understanding the effects of the mandatory provisions for Air Traffic Control Operator fatigue introduced in the EU in 2017 and in force since 2020, and provide insights on its efficiency and effectiveness. Responsibilities included qualitative and quantitative data collection and analysis, stakeholder engagement and the analysis of the situation of implementation in 2017, 2020, and currently, as well as the current effects of the regulation, also controlling for the possible effects of the COVID-19 pandemic that could distort the data.

  • Client: European Union Aviation Safety Agency (EASA)
  • Category: Air transport, Safety, Europe
See publication

Perspectives for the rolling stock supply in the EU

Assessment of the foreseeable demand and supply of rolling stock up to 2030 and the obstacles to the provision of rolling stock that may potentially hinder the attainment of EU goals in rail transport. The study provides the basis to consider whether or not there is a necessity to incentivise rolling stock supply within the EU and provides policy recommendations relevant to EU decision-making. Responsibilities and tasks included the elaboration of analyses related to the availability of financing mechanisms for the development, purchase, and retrofitting of rolling stock.

  • Client: European Parliament (Committee on Transport and Tourism)
  • Category: Railway transport, Europe
See publication
