About Me

MSc Engineering and Policy Analysis, specialized in Transport and Mobility.

Originally from Venezuela, I studied civil engineering in my home country but I realized by the time of my graduation that instead of the more traditional career path I was really passionate about using my skills and knowledge to drive societal impact, and after working for some time in consultancy with this end, I decided to pursue a master's degree in Engineering and Policy Analysis in TU Delft (the Netherlands).

I took a specialization in Transport and Mobility, an area I felt was very interesting for the challenges it presented for society and where I could help make a difference and have an engaging and challenging career to build a sustainable future. I am currently working at Ecorys Netherlands materializing this ambition, using my skills in data analytics, modeling, simulation, and strategic planning. You can find some of my projects below.

Energy transition of fisheries and aquaculture

European Commission (CINEA)

Analizing possible routes to achieve decarbonization of the European fisheries and aquaculture sector in the medium and long term in line with European carbon neutrality ambitions, with special attention to the impacts of recent crises on the sector, such as the COVID19 pandemic and the energy crisis. Responsibilities and tasks include planning and executing stakeholder consultation and analyzing the performance and current situation of the European fishing fleet to provide a point of reference to measure the decarbonization options.

Train drivers' training and certification

Revising the certification of train drivers aimed to enhance and facilitate their mobility across the rail network of the EU, as well as easing their assignment to operations in various Member States (for example, cross-border operations).
