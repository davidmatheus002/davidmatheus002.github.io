Transport emissions accounting

CountEmissions EU

Assessing policy proposals for a harmonized framework for transport emissions accounting in the EU for all modes of transportation with the goal of making the information available and comparable to enable future transport decarbonization policies. Tasks and responsibilities include aiding in the execution of stakeholder engagement activities and performing data analyses on their results. The policy proposal that stemmed from this impact assessment was included in the greening freight package of laws proposed by the European Commission.

  • Client: European Commission (DG MOVE)
  • Category: European Union, Transport, Decarbonization

Expected impacts of Fit for 55 legislation on connectivity and mobility in Europe

Based on a comprehensive review of eleven legislative texts of the Fit for 55 package with a transport policy dimension, this study highlights the expected impacts they may produce on connectivity and mobility, encompassing the concepts of efficiency and effectiveness in the ability of passengers and goods to travel within the EU. Tasks and responsibilities included analysis on cross-sectoral legislative texts from the Fit for 55 package and their impacts on connectivity and mobility for passengers and freight, stakeholder consultation, and issuing policy recommendations to the European Parliament.

Impact assessment for smart and flexible EU crewing rules in inland waterway transport

Crewing requirements in the EU are fragmented across river basins, and is heavily reliant on paper-based documentation that creates administrative burden and is prone to tampering. This impact assessment from DG MOVE considers policy options to address those issues. Task and responsibilities include evidence gathering to clearly delimit the problem, stakeholder engagement, quantitative and qualitative assessment of impacts, and assisting the client in answering emerging technical questions.

  • Client: European Commission (DG MOVE)
  • Category: Waterborne transport, IWT, future-proofing
See related publication

Scale up user-Centric and dAta driven soLutions for connEcted Urban Poles (SCALE-UP)

SCALE-UP is an EU-funded Innovation Action (Horizon 2020), focusing on 3 European urban nodes (Madrid, Antwerp, Turku) and exploring options to render them better connected and climate resilient while further developing complex multi-modal transport systems. As part of the project, we are looking into strategies better vertical integration between governance layers at city or Functional Urban Area, national, and TEN-T network levels.

  • Client: EU's HORIZON 2020 research and innovation programme
  • Category: TEN-T network, urban nodes, governance
See project website

Support to the preparation of Social Climate Plans

The Social Climate Fund is set up to support vulnerable households and small businesses in the transition to climate neutrality. To access funds from the SCF, Member States have to draw up Social Climate Plans by mid-2025. These plans should incorporate definitions, indicators and estimates for the affected vulnerable households and microenterprises in both the buildings and transport sectors, as well as outline measures in support of these vulnerable groups. As part of these contract for SG REFORM, we are assisting a number of Member States in drafting their Social Climate Plans. In particular, my work consists in identifying a list of potential measures and investments that can be matched to a Member State's specific conditions to address the undesired impacts on vulnerable groups, while addressing eligibility criteria and applying the Do no Significant Harm principle.

  • Client: European Commission (SG REFORM)
  • Category: Sustainable mobility, transport decarbonization, energy efficiency, climate finance
